It is a lazy evening. Maya likes to snuggle while she sees Paw Patrol and usually dozes off with her head against my ribs. She is still tiny enough that I can do a football carry. 

Dojo comes  to the drawing room wagging his tail. The wagging stops as he prepares to jump on to the sofa. He decides. He jumps. Feeling accomplished, the wagging resumes. He gives me a sniff and tries to steal a lick. I already knew he was going to try this and had turned away. He gets some of my hair instead of my chin. As he settles down, he carefully measures where, so that only his snout is on my thigh. Smart boy!

I feel for his ears. Find the little cartilagy thing where it attaches to his head and start rubbing it. This is our routine since I first discovered it. Dojo likes it so much that one of his legs involuntarily starts twitching.

He has been a lot of help. I like the new cartilagy ear bits as much as I liked yours.

Met Ma last week. I was at Kolkata for some business and I went over. She is doing well, yaar. Health wise, pretty much how you'd like to see it.

We talked about you. Recalled events from that day. She cried, I cried. We hugged. Baba was uncomfortable. He got up with a sigh, that I took to mean "I don't know what to do here. I'm sad too.", and left.

Baba has found a new football group. In continued observance of our ritual, I gifted him his annual tracks and shoes. He was excited to see a pocket on sleeve for his phone. Apparently he had dropped it and broken the screenguard in the last game.

Speaking about work, _that_ is _also_ okay. You know, not dry. I slowly drew myself back into it about 4 months ago. They were happy to have me back. A little sensitive around me. I can see a lot of hushed whispers stop when I enter a room or a common area. Kinda expected that. It has become a lot less than the first few days.

I'm not working on big projects. But honestly, I don't think I have the heart for it right now. Happy to work on tiny bits that are not time-critical. Pushing papers. Living the dream!

---

Well. On to **processing**. Lets move towards discussing the harder things. I've been told by the 12th self-help book I'm reading that this is the most important thing to be doing.

Kya hi bolun?

I miss you. I miss not having your blanket covered face be the first thing to see when I wake up. There's an emptiness there that just doesn't ... you know ... STOP.

I now realize that I was never _funny_. Haha. Just performing. You were my audience. The witness to my life. Now that there isn't a laugh to get paid with, I just don't perform. Pakka capitalist I am. See even in writing to you, I try so hard to be funny. 

Don't think I'm all _sad_, though. Maya and I have tons of fun. She is going through a giggling phase, and I tell you, yaar, those giggles are worth gold! It sounds like a thick soup bubbling over.

And she'll giggle even louder when I giggle back! It's pure joy. It is moments like those that draw a cartoonish outline around that emptiness I described. Makes everything _feel_ like it is going to be okay. Maybe better than okay.

<center>~</center>

Yeah, back to the processing now. What we had is not something I can imagine having with someone else. You know how when two colors mix on your pallette and you get a new color. You just can't do that with any other pair of colors. Not the same. You can get close. And close is _worth_ trying for.

My mom & dad keep throwing hints like they normally do. Asking me whether I want to see other people. And the gymnastics they go through to get the question out is a riot. We never spoke about hard things. And it shows through. I feel sorry for them. Haha.

But, yeah, about other people. Another pair of colors...

I honestly don't know. I just know I'm not ready right now. There is a lot of hurt I need to let scab over. And then pry open again with my nails. But hey, if that's not life, I dunno what is.

---

Its nearly a year and a half since we last met. I don't know how else to process not having your around. I don't know if I will stop. If I do, I don't know what I'll do with this diary. What I'll do with my fortnightly calendar invite..

I've worked with therapists before, but I'm finding their input insufficient. Hollow, even. I'm seeing the new guy tomorrow. I don't have my hopes up, but I guess even hollow help is ... help.

I'll tell you what it was like. See ya!
